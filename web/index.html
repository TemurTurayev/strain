How to play

Survive and spread: grow your colony, open a transmission window, and break out to a new host — before the immune system clears you, you kill the host, or 18 turns run out.

The four indicators

  • Colony load — your population. You need enough of it to transmit. Replicating grows it.
  • Host stability — the host's health. If it reaches zero you've killed your host before escaping, and you lose. Provoking lowers it.
  • Immune lock-on — how well the immune system has learned your signature. The higher it climbs, the harder it damages you every turn. This is the clock you're racing.
  • Transmission window — your escape gate. Closed by default; provoke opens it for a few turns. You can only transmit while it's open.

The four actions

  • Replicate — grow the colony, but you inflame the host and the immune system learns you faster.
  • Suppress — calm inflammation and lower immune lock-on, but you don't grow this turn (you lose tempo).
  • Provoke — trigger a symptom to open the transmission window for 3 turns. It costs host stability.
  • Transmit — break out to a new host. Works only while a window is open and your colony is large enough. This wins the run.

The tension

Grow loud and fast and the immune system locks on before you can escape. Stay too quiet and you run out of time. Each strain tilts this balance differently — Aggressive races, Silent hides, Sticky transmits early.
